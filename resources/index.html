Here are some resources I am grateful for.

resources & recommendations

science & activism

  • The Scientist Action and Advocacy Network is a New York-based group of scientists who provide pro bono research and data visualizations for partnering organizations. We've collaborated in the past with the Raise the Age NY campaign, the NYC Environmental Justice Alliance, and BagIt! NYC. Example projects: We launched a multimedia legislative campaigns to raise support for a 5-cent plastic bag fee in NYC. We provided a neuroscience research brief to legislators in Albany to support legislation to raise the age of criminal responsibility from 16 to 18 years of age (note: I am a prison abolitionist).
  • Science for the People is a magazine founded in 1969 out of the anti-war movement. It published until 1989 and was revived in 2015. The movement promotes a radical analysis of science through a decentralized and non-hierarchical governing structure.
